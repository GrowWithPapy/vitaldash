Accuracy

We use formulas from peer-reviewed sources: Mifflin-St Jeor (1990), Deurenberg (1991), ISSN (2017) protein recommendations, and CDC NHANES population data. These are reasonable estimates with known limits:

  • BMI cannot tell muscle from fat.
  • Body fat estimates from height and weight are approximations. A DEXA scan or hydrostatic test is more accurate.
  • TDEE depends on your daily activity, which is hard to measure honestly.
  • Goal projections assume a steady deficit or surplus, which most people do not maintain perfectly.

Treat the output as a starting point, not a prescription.
